# Example: Newsletter Brand

Sample run of `brand-launchpad` on a fictional weekly newsletter called
**The Cold Open** (one teardown of a great B2B onboarding flow per week).

> All names, numbers, and outputs are illustrative only.

---

## Step 0: Capture

```yaml
project_name: the-cold-open
brand_type: newsletter
real_name_or_alias: "@thecoldopen"
one_sentence_what_it_does: "One annotated teardown of a great B2B onboarding flow, every Thursday."
icp:
  who: B2B product + growth people who own activation
  pain_or_desire: "Good onboarding" advice is generic; they want to see real flows, annotated
  where_they_hang_out: LinkedIn, X, Lenny's community, email
desired_outcome_12mo: 10,000 subscribers + 40% open rate
constraints:
  anonymous_required: no
  time_per_week_hours: 8
  camera_comfort: comfortable
  platforms_committed: [Email, LinkedIn, X]
```

## Step 3: Positioning sentence

> The Cold Open is the only onboarding newsletter that shows you the actual
> screens — annotated frame by frame — instead of telling you to "reduce
> friction".

(Counterposition: the category is wall-to-wall abstract advice. The
through-line is *artifacts over adjectives* — every issue is a real flow,
not a listicle.)

## Step 5: Hero line candidates

| Candidate | Visualize | Falsify | Nobody else |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| "The onboarding flows you wish you'd designed, annotated every Thursday." | Pass | Pass | Pass |
| "Actionable insights to improve your activation funnel." | Fail | Fail | Fail |
| "Stop reading onboarding tips. Start reading onboarding screens." | Pass | Pass | Pass |

Winner: candidate 3. Falsifiable (it really is screens, not tips),
visualizable (the reader pictures an annotated flow), and it directly
counterpositions the entire "tips" category nobody else will attack.

## Step 6c: Named recurring series

1. **"The teardown"** — the Thursday flagship issue: one real onboarding
   flow, annotated screen by screen. This IS the source artifact.
2. **"One screen"** — Monday LinkedIn carousel pulling a single screen from
   that week's teardown with the one lesson. Derivative of the teardown.
3. **"Readers' flows"** — Monthly issue tearing down a subscriber's own
   onboarding (submitted via reply). Turns the audience into the pipeline
   and creates a reason to reply, which trains the inbox placement.

## Step 6b: Source-artifact pipeline (newsletter-critical)

The Thursday teardown is the one real artifact. From it:
- 1 LinkedIn carousel ("One screen", Monday)
- 1 X thread (the 5 sharpest annotations)
- 2 short X posts (the single most surprising finding + a contrarian take)
- 1 reply-bait question for the next "Readers' flows"

One teardown → six surfaces. Never write a "content" piece that doesn't
trace back to a teardown.

## Step 9: Day-21 milestone

Land one teardown of a *recognizable* product (one the audience already
uses) by Day 21 — ideally with a quote or correction from someone on that
product's team. That single issue is the credibility anchor every later
growth push points back to.

## Newsletter-specific notes

- **Cadence floor matters more than ceiling.** A newsletter brand dies from
  a missed Thursday faster than a video brand dies from a missed Reel.
  `operating-rules.md` sets the "ship the teardown even if the carousel
  slips" rule.
- **The B-side handle is the archive.** Main = email + the polished
  carousel; `@thecoldopen.notes` is the working file ("3 flows I rejected
  this week and why"). Keeps the main feed scarce and the lab visible.
